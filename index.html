About Me

I’m a digital creator with a BSc (Hons) in Sound Engineering and Multimedia, specialising in learning technology. I design interactive, accessible learning activities that combine instructional design, UX (WCAG/PSBAR compliance), and audio/video production, working closely with subject matter experts to bring their ideas to life.

I have spent the past decade working within the award-winning HELM team (Health e-Learning & Media) in the School of Health Sciences at The University of Nottingham, where I have collaborated with students, practitioners, and educators to co-create innovative digital learning solutions. I hold a PRINCE2 certification, enabling me to deliver complex projects efficiently and with precision, and I’m a Certified Member of Association of Learning Technologists (CMALT), demonstrating my ongoing commitment to professional practice in digital learning.

Video Production

I have extensive experience in filming and editing educational videos that are integrated into interactive learning packages. These resources enhance student engagement, support multimodal learning, and provide flexible opportunities for knowledge acquisition, while aligning with accessibility best practice. Many resources are released under a Creative Commons licence for worldwide access.

In line with the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy's Redefining Physiotherapy Education, I collaborated on redesigning the Undergraduate Physiotherapy Curriculum. We developed six interactive, problem-based learning scenarios for 120+ first-year students, each starting with an engaging video stimulus — embedded directly within Moodle — and progressing to small-group discussions and interactive online activities. Completion tracking was built into the pathway, ensuring students could not advance to the next activity until they had fully completed and achieved the prior one, maintaining both learning integrity and consistent progression. Feedback has been positive, with case studies revised based on student input.

I also produce short-form “learning shorts” optimised for platforms such as YouTube and social media, applying microlearning principles for accessible, on-the-go content that complements longer resources.

Virtual Placement

Students on a wide range of courses, particularly in healthcare, are required to complete placement learning. Placement learning supports specific learning outcomes and is a requirement for successful course completion at the University. However, placement experiences are becoming increasingly difficult to source and more expensive to resource. The pandemic posed a particular challenge, and availability has not fully returned to pre-pandemic levels. To address this, a bespoke "Virtual Placement" (VP) platform was designed to provide authentic, practice-oriented experiences. Students are introduced to their practice supervisor, Krish, who guides them along their learning journey, including virtual home visits to a patient, Lionel.

The VP platform applies experiential learning principles and incorporates feedback loops that replicate guidance from clinical mentors. A key feature is the integration of the OpenAI API to power an AI-driven practice assessor, available to students 24/7 — providing on-demand guidance and feedback at any point in their learning journey. The chatbot was deliberately scoped and restricted to remain relevant only to the subject matter of the placement, ensuring a focused and educationally sound experience. Built using a robust technology stack – including PHP, HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, AJAX, jQuery, jQuery UI, Bootstrap, JSON, MySQL, OpenID Connect, OAuth 2.0, TinyMCE, Apache ECharts, and RSS – the platform delivers a rich, interactive, and secure virtual learning experience. Accessibility audits using WAVE, axe DevTools, and Silktide plug-ins ensure compliance and usability for diverse learners.

A built-in learning dashboard enables students to self-assess and track their development across key clinical competencies at three structured points throughout the experience — at the start, mid-point, and end of the placement. These self-assessments are mapped directly to the Common Placement Assessment Form (CPAF) 2024, the nationally recognised framework used by healthcare programmes to evaluate student competency in real-world placements. By mirroring this framework within the virtual environment, students arrive at their physical placements with a clear, data-informed picture of their own strengths and development areas. The dashboard visualises this progression over time, giving both students and educators meaningful analytics to support learning conversations and identify where additional support may be needed.

Rollout of the platform was carefully managed to ensure genuine curriculum integration. Rather than simply making it available online, the platform was embedded directly into Year 1 lectures, with sessions delivered in person to introduce students to the experience and set expectations. Completion of the Virtual Placement was made compulsory prior to students attending their first real-world placement, positioning it as a meaningful preparatory step rather than an optional resource. This approach has now been rolled out across four student cohorts (400+ users), with the platform continuing to evolve based on ongoing student and staff feedback.

ADHD Translation Resources

This project focused on equipping healthcare professionals and members of the public with accessible information on how to assess and recognise ADHD in both children and adults. The resources equip learners with an understanding of ADHD as well as the role of primary care professionals in its diagnosis and management and are endorsed by the Royal College of General Practitioners.

These were split into two resources, Understanding ADHD and The Role of the General Practitioner in ADHD Diagnosis and Management, which were translated from English into French, Spanish, and German. This involved close collaboration with stakeholders and professional translators, as well as accessibility audits using WAVE, Silktide, and axe DevTools to ensure content is clear, inclusive, and usable for diverse audiences. The RLOs were technically created using HTML, CSS, PHP, and Java, combining educational content with interactive digital functionality to enhance learner engagement.

Commercial Work

St Barnabas Hospice – Palliative & End of Life Care Learning

St Barnabas Hospice hired me to digitise their face-to-face palliative and end of life care training into commercially available, sellable learning packages — extending their expertise beyond the hospice and into the wider healthcare sector. A core design challenge was transforming content traditionally delivered across two full days of face-to-face training into something accessible and sustainable for busy healthcare professionals. Drawing on Cognitive Load Theory, the content was deliberately chunked into bite-sized modules that learners can complete at their own pace, reducing the risk of cognitive overload and allowing time for reflection between sessions. Each course is structured across four modules, designed for skills-based, role-relevant learning that translates authentic clinical knowledge into an accessible online format. Key concepts are introduced early in the package and revisited at later stages — a principle known as Spaced Repetition — which research consistently shows improves long-term retention and the transfer of learning into practice. To bring case study patients to life during the drafting phase, AI-generated video was used to create realistic patient personas, allowing stakeholders to visualise and review the learner experience early in the development process before full production. Progression through each module is gated, meaning learners must complete and demonstrate understanding of each activity before advancing — a principle rooted in Bloom's Mastery Learning theory, which holds that learners should achieve competency at each stage before building on it. This scaffolded approach, also aligned with Gagné's Conditions of Learning, ensures that knowledge is sequenced intentionally and that foundational understanding is secured before more complex concepts are introduced.

The packages are deployed on The Skills Network's EQUAL platform, a recognised LMS for workforce development in health and social care. Platform constraints required the content to be built natively in HTML5, React.js, and CSS rather than traditional SCORM authoring tools — a technical challenge that demanded both instructional design rigour and custom development to deliver a polished, interactive learner experience.

This project demonstrates my ability to work with subject matter experts to translate complex, sensitive subject matter into structured digital learning, whilst adapting to real-world platform and technical constraints to deliver a commercially viable product.
