About Me

I’m a digital creator with a BSc (Hons) in Sound Engineering and Multimedia, specialising as a Learning Technologist. My expertise spans instructional design, accessibility and UX (WCAG/PSBAR compliance), and audio/video production. I collaborate with subject matter experts to design and deliver interactive, engaging learning experiences.

Alongside my creative and technical expertise, I hold a PRINCE2 certification in project management and am a Certified Member of ALT (CMALT), evidencing my commitment to professional practice in learning technology. I am also trained in Mental Health First Aid, which supports my ability to foster inclusive and supportive learning environments.

Audio Engineer

I am a qualified sound engineer with a BSc (Hons), and I currently lead production on the "It's Notts Just Physio" podcast. This project has been instrumental in bridging the gap between theory and practice within the BSc Physiotherapy curriculum, while also serving as a platform for staff onboarding and continuing professional development. The series is available on all streaming platforms.

In terms of learning design, the podcast follows key educational principles. Episodes are kept concise and structured to avoid overwhelming learners, with supplementary visuals and transcripts provided to support multimodal engagement. By embedding storytelling and narrative learning, the podcast creates relevance and meaningful connection, helping students link academic knowledge to professional practice.

Video Production

I have extensive experience in filming and editing educational videos that are integrated into interactive learning packages. These resources are designed to enhance student engagement, support multimodal learning, and provide flexible opportunities for knowledge acquisition within the curriculum, while aligning with accessibility best practice. Many of these resources are released as open educational resources under a Creative Commons licence, allowing them to be accessed and reused by learners and educators worldwide.

In line with the principles highlighted by the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy in Redefining Physiotherapy Education, I collaborated with content authors to redesign the Undergraduate Physiotherapy Curriculum. We developed six interactive, problem-based learning scenarios for over 120 first-year students, each starting with an engaging video stimulus, progressing to small-group discussions, and concluding with structured, interactive activities on the virtual learning environment. These resources were carefully aligned with adult learning principles to foster active engagement, self-directed learning, and the practical application of theoretical knowledge. Feedback has been overwhelmingly positive, with some case studies revised based on student input. Trends suggest improvements in both grade outcomes and National Student Survey (NSS) satisfaction scores following the curriculum redesign.

In addition, I produce short-form content to create concise and digestible learning materials. These “learning shorts” are optimised for platforms such as YouTube and social media, applying microlearning principles to provide students with accessible, on-the-go content that complements longer-form resources.



Web Design

Design of a bespoke "Virtual Placement" VLE in response to shortage of student placements. Students are introduced to their practice supervisor Krish who guides them along their learning journey where they will complete home visits to a virtual patient Lionel.

This project applies experiential learning principles and incorporates feedback loops that replicate the guidance of mentors in clinical placements. It leverages AJAX, jQuery, JavaScript, PHP, CSS, and HTML to create a rich, interactive learning experience. The project has now been rolled out to three student cohorts, with further development shaped by their feedback. In Summer 2025, I conducted an accessibility audit using the WAVE, axe DevTools and Silktide plug-ins to ensure compliance and enhance usability.
