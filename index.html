02 — Video

Video Production

I have extensive experience in filming and editing educational videos that are integrated into interactive learning packages. These resources enhance student engagement, support multimodal learning, and provide flexible opportunities for knowledge acquisition, while aligning with accessibility best practice. Many resources are released under a Creative Commons licence for worldwide access.

In line with the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy's Redefining Physiotherapy Education, I collaborated on redesigning the Undergraduate Physiotherapy Curriculum. We developed six interactive, problem-based learning scenarios for 120+ first-year students, each starting with an engaging video stimulus — embedded directly within Moodle — and progressing to small-group discussions and interactive online activities. Completion tracking was built into the pathway, ensuring students could not advance to the next activity until they had fully completed and achieved the prior one. Feedback has been positive, with case studies revised based on student input.

I also produce short-form "learning shorts" optimised for platforms such as YouTube and social media, applying microlearning principles for accessible, on-the-go content that complements longer resources.

Virtual Placement

Students on a wide range of courses, particularly in healthcare, are required to complete placement learning. Placement experiences are becoming increasingly difficult to source and more expensive to resource. To address this, a bespoke "Virtual Placement" (VP) platform was designed to provide authentic, practice-oriented experiences. Students are introduced to their practice supervisor, Krish, who guides them along their learning journey, including virtual home visits to a patient, Lionel.

A built-in learning dashboard enables students to self-assess and track their development across key clinical competencies at three structured points — at the start, mid-point, and end of the placement. These self-assessments are mapped directly to the Common Placement Assessment Form (CPAF) 2024, the nationally recognised framework used by healthcare programmes to evaluate student competency in real-world placements. The dashboard visualises progression over time, giving both students and educators meaningful analytics to support learning conversations.

Rollout was carefully managed to ensure genuine curriculum integration. The platform was embedded directly into Year 1 lectures, with sessions delivered in person. Completion was made compulsory prior to students attending their first real-world placement. This approach has been rolled out across four student cohorts (400+ users). Accessibility audits using WAVE, axe DevTools, and Silktide ensure compliance for diverse learners.

VP – AI Practice Assessor

The virtual practice assessor — powered by the OpenAI API — enables students to access support 24/7, replicating the availability of a real clinical mentor without the resource overhead. The chatbot was deliberately constrained to the subject matter of the placement, preventing off-topic responses and ensuring every interaction reinforces relevant learning.

This is a practical demonstration of AI being used responsibly in education: not to replace human interaction, but to extend its availability and scale its impact.

St Barnabas Hospice – Palliative & End of Life Care Learning

Cognitive Load Theory Spaced Repetition Bloom's Mastery Learning Gagné's Conditions of Learning

St Barnabas Hospice engaged me to digitise their face-to-face palliative and end of life care training into commercially available, sellable learning packages — extending their expertise beyond the hospice and into the wider healthcare sector. A core design challenge was transforming content traditionally delivered across two full days of face-to-face training into something accessible and sustainable for busy healthcare professionals.

Drawing on Cognitive Load Theory, the content was deliberately chunked into bite-sized modules learners can complete at their own pace, reducing cognitive overload and allowing time for reflection. Each course is structured across four modules. Key concepts are introduced early and revisited at later stages — a principle known as Spaced Repetition — which research consistently shows improves long-term retention and the transfer of learning into practice.

To bring case study patients to life during the drafting phase, AI-generated video was used to create realistic patient personas, allowing stakeholders to visualise and review the learner experience early in development. Progression through each module is gated — rooted in Bloom's Mastery Learning — meaning learners must demonstrate understanding before advancing. This scaffolded approach, also aligned with Gagné's Conditions of Learning, ensures knowledge is sequenced intentionally.

The packages are deployed on The Skills Network's EQUAL platform, a recognised LMS for workforce development in health and social care. Platform constraints required the content to be built natively in HTML5, React.js, and CSS rather than traditional SCORM authoring tools — a technical challenge that demanded both instructional design rigour and custom development to deliver a polished, interactive learner experience.

This project demonstrates my ability to work with subject matter experts to translate complex, sensitive subject matter into structured digital learning, whilst adapting to real-world platform and technical constraints to deliver a commercially viable product.
